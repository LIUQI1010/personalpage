# .impeccable.md — Design Context for Qi Liu's Personal Portfolio

## Design Context

### Users
Recruiters and hiring managers in cloud/tech, fellow developers, and potential collaborators. They arrive with a task in mind (evaluate Qi's skills), but the site should make them forget that task momentarily — pulling them into exploring rather than just scanning. The ideal visit feels like discovery, not a resume review.

### Brand Personality
**Three words: Playful, Experimental, Star**

Qi Liu is the star — not in an egotistical way, but in the sense of someone with genuine presence and craft. The site should feel like it was made by a person with taste and curiosity, not assembled by a tool. Every detail should signal human intention: deliberate choices, unexpected moments, things that make you smile or lean in.

The NZ star-field background is not a generic "space" metaphor — it's the shape of New Zealand, a personal and geographic signature. This specificity is the brand: personal, rooted, and quietly meaningful.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Dark-only.** The deep space blue (`oklch(0.13 0.03 261.7)`) is non-negotiable — it sets the mood and frames everything else.
- **Primary accent:** green → blue gradient (`from-green-400 to-blue-500`). Alive, technical, slightly cosmic.
- **NOT AI-looking.** Avoid: generic glassmorphism stacks, cookie-cutter card grids, over-polished "SaaS dashboard" vibes, symmetrical layouts that feel templated. Lean into irregularity, craft, and handmade-feeling details (the SVG signature animation is a perfect example of this).
- **Curious, not overwhelming.** Reveal things gradually. Let interactions reward exploration. Don't front-load everything.
- **No anti-references given** — trust the existing direction; the goal is to deepen it, not redirect it.

### Design Principles

1. **Human over polished.** Prefer choices that feel deliberate and personal over ones that feel "professionally safe." A slightly unexpected layout beats a perfectly centered one.

2. **Reward exploration.** Hide small delights in interactions (mouse trail, signature animation, scroll reveals). The site should have more to give the longer you stay.

3. **The NZ specificity rule.** Personal details (the NZ map, the handwritten signature, geographic identity) are features, not decoration. Lean into them — they're what make this site feel human rather than AI-generated.

4. **Motion with purpose.** GSAP animations exist to guide attention and add life, not to show off. If an animation doesn't serve discovery or delight, cut it or simplify it.

5. **Dark and deliberate.** Every element sits against a deep background that demands contrast and intention. Avoid filling space — let the dark breathe. Sparse is better than busy.
